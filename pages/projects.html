Research and Projects

I am not a trained economist, but below are some notes on some topics that interest me. All comments are welcome.
Projects with Drafts

  • Demarcation

    I argue that in the social sciences, the effect of a theory becoming known by the agents it purports to describe amounts to a problem of selection- not on the data which one might test the theory against, but on the process governing the generation of any possible data. Under this interpretation, it becomes unclear when one should regard a social or economic theory as testable. I propose a criterion for classifying testable social and economic theories as an extension of Karl Popper's answer to the Problem of Demarcation.

  • What was the Price of Slaves in Ancient Rome?

    The paucity of numerical data need not result in scholars implementing ad hoc empirical methods in their study of ancient economies. Using the method of partial identification, I give an example of how regression analysis may be implemented in the study of ancient economies, and show that this method allows scholars to explicitly demonstrate the effect on their conclusions of their assumptions regarding the representativeness of archeological evidence.

  • Algorithms for Predicting the Outcomes of Over-Under Wagers

    (website coming soon) A.J. Hesby and I are developing a code base for fine-tuning predictive models of Over-Under wagers. We are also working on developing a website containing full code documentation and comprehensive discussions on the construction and implementation of our models.

Prospective Topics/Projects without Drafts

  • Does the Fed affect Tactit Collusion?

    The basic theory of oligopolistic competition implies that a firm's decision to maintain tacit collusion is a function of the risk free rate. I study the connection between a central banks influence over interest rates and the incidence of tacit collusion across an economy.

  • Partially Idenitifying the Spillover Effects of Minimum Wage Policy

    The direct effect of minimum wage policy on poverty and unemployment is controversial both theoretically and empirically. I study whether minimum wage policy influences the structure of wages in the immediate neighborhood of a newly establsihed wage floor. Once a new policy is established, changes in the structure around the new minimum are likely to influence the propsective earnings of workers who were working at or near this wage level. Hence, minium wage policy may have indirect effects on unemployment and poverty if it affects the wage structure locally.

  • Leveraging Uncertainty in Risk Management

    Many models of risk management utilize distributional assumptions on the risk associated with porfolios. In particular, FHVaR utilizes assumptions on the stability of the distribution of returns over time. When these assumptions are relaxed, the VaR and resulting level of margin to be allocated becomes uncertain. However, the determination of margin by risk managers based on an uncertain calculation of the FHVaR may actually benefit trading firms in the long run depending on the decison rule used for allocating this margin.
