Lecture Notes

These are some of the best notes that I have used for learning various topics.

Even though they are all available online, there is little chance you'd have come across them unless you scoured these writers' websites or took one of their classes. There are many lecture notes online (especially in math), so I am attempting to reserve this page for notes that I have used, and that I found to be very user-friendly.

Mathematics

  • Sequences and Series by Santiago Cañez (Northwestern)
  • Linear Algebra by Santiago Cañez (Northwestern)
  • Multivariable Calculus (291-2/291-3) by Santiago Cañez (Northwestern)

Economics

  • Logit (book chapter) by Kenneth E. Train (Berkeley)
  • Intro & Advanced Econometrics Topics (slides) by Michael Gmeiner (LSE)
  • Econometrics (Probability, Asymptotics) by Ludvig Sinander (Oxford)
