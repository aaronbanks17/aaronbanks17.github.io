Methodology Readings

I borrow the concept of a methodological work from Gilboa et al. (2022). Below is a list of works that I think are methodologically interesting. This latter classification is independent of what I might ultimately think about the individual works or their methodological contributions. I hope to be updating this list consistently.

Articles

  • The Use of Knowledge in Society by Friedrich Hayek
  • The Utility Analysis of Choices Involving Risk by Milton Friedman & Leonard J. Savage
  • Uncertainty, Evolution, and Economic Theory by Armen A. Alchian
  • A Difficulty in the Concept of Social Welfare by Kenneth J. Arrow
  • Bandwagon and Underdog Effects and the Possibility of Election Predictions by Herbert A. Simon
  • The Predictability of Social Events by Emilie Grunberg & Franco Modigliani
  • The Methodology of Positive Economics by Milton Freidman
  • Irrational Behavior and Economic Theory by Gary S. Becker
  • Econometric Policy Evaluation: A Critique by Robert E. Lucas
  • Identification of Endogenous Social Effects: The Reflection Problem by Charles F. Manski
  • Falsifiability by Wojciech Olzewski & Alvaro Sandroni

Books

  • War and Peace by Leo Tolstoy
  • Risk, Uncertainty and Profit by Frank H. Knight
  • Human Action (vol. 1) by Ludwig Von Mises
  • The Logic of Scientific Discovery by Karl R. Popper
  • Statistical Decision Functions by Abraham Wald
  • Statistical Decision Theory by James O. Berger
  • Identification for Prediction and Decision by Charles F. Manski
  • Theory of Decision Under Uncertainty by Itzhak Gilboa
  • Robustness by Lars Peter Hansen and Thomas J. Sargent
